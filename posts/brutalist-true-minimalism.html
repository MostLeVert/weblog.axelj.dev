Brutalist True-Minimalism

Introduction

Brutalist true-minimalism provides a novel combination of two well known paradigms of design, brutalism and minimalism, it seeks to reinvigorate the original motives of those philosophies. Brutalist true-minimalism is a critique of the direction modern minimalism and brutalism have taken through the integration of superfluous elements that violate the ethos. Modern brutalist houses tend to actually be more minimalist than the modern minimalists house -- but they introduce architectural elements that are nothing more than designs; not bad, but certainly not in line with the original movement. While modern brutalism has maintained its roots, modern minimalism has almost entirely departed from the original ethos. Through the rabid commodification of the principles the word and ethos have become watered down and ubiquitous. I'm not against progression of movements, I just seek to extract the ultimate value in the distillation of the ethos of the two philosophies at their confluence. To summarize the reason for my founding of brutalist true-minimalism I'd like to say: The modern minimalist's house useless trinkets, or design elements, likes stones or books that haven't been read -- it hides the functional elements of a building in the walls, pushing their conscience, both as their own, and in the owner, into the edges of the building. This contributes to a great clutter. Meanwhile the modern brutalist house also hides those, what are in the modern world considered nuisances within the walls or floors, the modern brutalist introduces unnecessary architectural elements for the sake of having sharp lines. This is because the modern brutalist is afraid of the true reality of brutalism as a stark, depressing, purely functional entity. Brutalist true-minimalism pulls the airy, friendly ethos of minimalism together with the pure function of brutalism, and in doing so, interesting phenomena of design arise, e.g. in brutalist true-minimalism the pipes and wiring of a building must be exposed on the interior and meticulously organized to bring out their pure function, and by not hiding them, the true-minimalism of it is exposed. In this way the brutalist true-minimalist will reduce their unnecessary bathrooms, electrical outlets, etc., a key part of the unique, novel, aspect of this confluence. We can observe new things arise from known things; which, as an anecdote, is the function of the human mind.
